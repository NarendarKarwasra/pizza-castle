Cold-Coffe

Cold coffee, also known as iced coffee or cold brew coffee, is a refreshing and invigorating beverage that has become a popular choice, especially in warm weather or for those seeking a chilled alternative to hot coffee. This beverage is made from coffee that is brewed hot and then cooled down or from coffee that is brewed with cold water over an extended period, known as cold brewing. Iced coffee can be enjoyed in various forms. One common method involves brewing hot coffee and then pouring it over ice, often with added milk or cream and sweeteners. Cold brew coffee, on the other hand, is made by steeping coarsely ground coffee beans in cold water for an extended period, typically 12-24 hours. The result is a smoother and less acidic coffee concentrate that can be diluted with water or milk and customized to taste. Iced coffee offers a delightful contrast between the robust flavor of coffee and the chill of ice, making it a popular choice for a pick-me-up in the morning or a refreshing afternoon treat. It's often enjoyed in coffee shops.
