Noodles

Noodles are a diverse and universally enjoyed staple in the world of cuisine, with a history dating back thousands of years. These versatile and satisfying food items consist of unleavened dough, typically made from wheat, rice, or other grains, that is rolled out and cut into various shapes and sizes. Noodles are an essential component of many global cuisines, from Italian pasta to Asian noodle dishes, each with its own unique preparation and cultural significance. In Italy, pasta is the quintessential noodle variety, coming in an array of shapes and textures, from spaghetti to penne, and from lasagna to ravioli. Italian pasta is often served with a wide range of sauces, such as tomato-based marinara, creamy Alfredo, or herb-infused pesto. In Asian cuisine, noodles take on an extensive array of forms and preparations, such as Chinese lo mein, Japanese udon, or Thai pad Thai. They are commonly made from wheat, rice, or starches like sweet potato, and are frequently used in soups, stir-fries, and cold salads.
